Batya Segal

Co-Founder & General Manager

A proud daughter of Jerusalem, Batya was born into an orthodox Jewish family with rich Yemenite heritage. Growing up with four siblings in a city steeped in history, her early years were marked by the Six-Day War (1967) and the Yom Kippur War (1973).

Following a supernatural encounter as a young mother, Batya underwent profound spiritual transformation, becoming a believer in Yeshua.

Batya met Barry at a weekly gathering for songwriters and musicians in Jerusalem. United by shared love for music and vision for their homeland, they married in 1987.

In the wake of the Intifada, witnessing increasing hardships in Israeli society, Batya and Barry felt called to offer relief. Vision for Israel was born in 1994.

Barry Segal

Co-Founder & President

Born in Detroit, Michigan, Barry Segal's life has been marked by transformative moments. Raised in a Jewish American family, he discovered his musical talents early—guitar at age 10, clarinet at 12.

In 1971, Barry found salvation through Yeshua (Jesus). This encounter brought profound change, shifting his focus to praise and worship music.

Barry made aliyah in 1981, moving to Jerusalem with steadfast commitment to his faith. There he met Batya, forming a partnership in music and ministry.

The vision that changed everything: Barry received a powerful prophetic vision, like Joseph's interpretation of Pharaoh's dreams. He foresaw Israel experiencing times of plenty followed by great distress, prompting the establishment of Vision for Israel in 1994.

The Founding Story

How Vision for Israel was born from prophecy and forged through tragedy

1993 - THE VISION

A Prophetic Warning

Barry Segal received a powerful vision. He saw that Israel would experience a time of great plenty—followed by a period of great struggle.

Like Joseph interpreting Pharaoh's dreams, Barry understood that something had to be done to prepare—to provide for the poor and needy, who would be hit hardest by the instability.

1994 - FOUNDATION

Vision for Israel is Born

Along with his wife Batya, Barry founded Vision for Israel with this lofty mission in mind.

This young ministry began reaching out and offering financial aid to poor and needy families around Israel. It was a grassroots movement driven by faith and a divine mandate to serve.

1996 - TRAGEDY STRIKES

Personal Loss

A couple of short years later, the story took a tragic turn. The Segal family was touched by the pain that affected many Israelis.

Barry and Batya's daughter lost three teenage friends in a bus attacked by a Hamas suicide bomber. Another girl who served in one of the platoons she instructed was also killed in a separate attack on the same bus route—buses she had ridden each week.

PASSOVER SEASON

Turning Pain into Purpose

During Passover season, Barry and Batya responded not with bitterness, but with compassion. They began assisting the families torn apart by terrorism, embodying their commitment to serve Israel in its darkest hours.

This tragedy deepened VFI's mission—transforming personal loss into a legacy of hope and restoration for thousands of families affected by violence.

FOLLOWING YEARS

Expanding the Mission

In the following years, Vision for Israel expanded to include other desperately needed humanitarian services.

The ministry now serves Holocaust survivors, at-risk kids, and many others in need of help throughout Israel—continuing the prophetic vision that started it all.

From Prophecy to Purpose

What began as a prophetic vision in 1993 and was tested by tragedy in 1996 has become a 30-year legacy of faithfulness. Vision for Israel stands today as proof that even in our darkest moments, God can use our pain to bring light and hope to an entire nation.

Historical Context

The First Intifada Era

1987-1993: A period of intense conflict and instability in Israel

The Crisis:

During the First Intifada, Israel faced unprecedented challenges. Bus bombings, suicide attacks, and ongoing violence created an atmosphere of fear and uncertainty. Families who had once felt secure suddenly found themselves vulnerable.

Impact: The poor, elderly, and needy were hit hardest - unable to afford security measures or relocate to safer areas.

VFI's Response:

When the Segal family personally experienced loss through terrorism, they didn't retreat—they responded with even greater compassion. They began assisting families torn apart by violence, providing:

  • Emergency financial aid
  • Food and essential supplies
  • Emotional and spiritual support
  • Long-term care for victims' families

From personal tragedy came a commitment to serve Israel in its darkest hours - embodying true resilience and faith.
